Don't know what to write?

You can answer these basic mental health concerns

Mood Changes:

  • Persistent sadness, hopelessness, or feeling "empty"
  • Irritability, agitation, or anger outbursts
  • Mood swings or sudden shifts in emotions

    • Anxiety Symptoms:

  • Excessive worry or fear
  • Feeling restless or on edge
  • Panic attacks, including rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, or difficulty breathing

    • Cognitive Symptoms:

  • Difficulty concentrating or focusing
  • Racing thoughts or inability to slow down mental activity
  • Memory problems or forgetfulness

    • Behavioral Changes:

  • Changes in appetite or weight (significant increase or decrease)
  • Sleep disturbances, such as insomnia or oversleeping
  • Avoidance of social interactions or withdrawal from activities you once enjoyed

    • Physical Symptoms:

  • Unexplained aches and pains
  • Fatigue or lack of energy, even after rest
  • Digestive problems or headaches without a clear medical cause

    • Substance Use:

  • Increased use of alcohol, drugs, or other substances as a coping mechanism
  • Difficulty cutting down or controlling substance use despite negative consequences

    • Relationship Issues:

  • Strained relationships with family, friends, or coworkers
  • Difficulty communicating or connecting with others
  • Feeling isolated or misunderstood

    • Suicidal Thoughts or Behaviors:

  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide, even if they're fleeting
  • Making plans or attempts to harm yourself
  • Feeling like life is not worth living or that others would be better off without you

    • Psychotic Symptoms (in severe cases):

  • Hallucinations (seeing or hearing things that aren't there)
  • Delusions (strongly held false beliefs)
  • Disorganized thinking or speech
